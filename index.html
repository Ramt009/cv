Mart Regunton

Repetition achieves mastery triumphantly

I started climbing in January 2017 when I joined a meetup at a bouldering gym. I quickly progressed from V2s to V7s within one year. In June, 2018 I climbed outdoors for the first time at Tramway (San Jacinto Mountains) and sent my first V4 boulder called Square Dance. In 2019, I attempted to climb a lot of boulders, anywhere from V0s all the way to a V8. The only notable climb I was able to send that season was a roof climb V4 called Standing On The Head Of The Dragon. During the pandemic and quarantine period that dominated most of 2020, I did not climb outdoors. I was only able to climb indoors for short periods of time, however I felt stronger than ever and sent multiple V8s. On August 14, 2021, I went outdoors again and to my surprise, sent Swing Dance, a popular benchmark V7 boulder. Feeling very ecstatic that my training has finally paid, I decided to document my climbing journey to record my progression, training, technique and strength.
